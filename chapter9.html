Chapter 9 Study Guide

Cloud Computing: Why Buy When You Can Rent?

This chapter explains how cloud computing changes how firms access technology by allowing them to rent computing resources instead of owning them.

What to Know

Cloud computing allows firms to access computing resources on demand instead of building their own infrastructure.

Main Theme

The shift from ownership to renting technology lowers costs, increases flexibility, and enables faster innovation.

Manager Takeaway

Managers must evaluate cost, scalability, and risk when deciding between cloud and traditional IT infrastructure.

Vocabulary

Cloud Computing

Definition: Cloud computing is the delivery of computing services over the internet.

Why it matters: Firms can avoid large upfront investments and instead pay for what they use.

Utility Computing

Definition: Computing resources are treated like utilities (electricity or water).

Why it matters: Firms only pay for usage instead of owning infrastructure.

SaaS (Software as a Service)

Definition: Software delivered via the internet instead of installed locally.

Example: Google Docs

IaaS

Definition: Renting servers, storage, and networking resources.

PaaS

Definition: Platforms for developers to build applications.

Scalability

Definition: Ability to increase or decrease resources quickly.

Elasticity

Definition: Automatic scaling based on demand.

Downtime

Definition: When systems are unavailable.

Vendor Lock-In

Definition: Difficulty switching providers.
